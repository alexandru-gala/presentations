07 · Lesson 1 — determinism

Left alone, AI tightens the gates too hard.

Real example, from intake: the multi-adversarial review took 2-3 open questions from the PRD and turned them into 15-20 questions and risks sent to the gate.
“POST /accept uses an action verb in the URL ([HARD] rule 3).”
“7 of 8 reviewers returned NO-GO (only kimi/Android = GO).”
“HALT short-circuits the judges; 9 null dims synthesized. No reviewer spend.”
— real bouncer verdicts, mined from the gate runs
How to get through
Answer the questions and risks as they show up — don't let them pile up in the pack.
Manage ambiguity before intake — as much as you possibly can.
And definitely close them before the gate — the bouncer counts every open question.
Let AI design its own deterministic steps and they come out too strict and complex: rules that block people in the middle of a working session.
08 · Lesson 2 — boundaries

Without clear boundaries, agents help each other… too much.

Non-deterministic steps, with loose guardrails, allow mixed ownership inside a multi-step orchestration.

Real example, from testing: the QA agent (inspector-gadget) noticed the backend services weren't running. Instead of telling the orchestrator or the backend agent, it started them itself.

Helpful in the moment — but on the next run, who owns the environment?

09 · One rule

Choose your path instead of letting the AI choose it for you!

Deterministic
Strict
Gates, verdicts, handoff schemas — where repeatability matters.
›
Non-deterministic
Loose
Research, design, code — where AI has to judge.
›
Always yours
Human
Your call
Review everything agents propose. Stop at every unexpected behavior — or the process stays broken for the next person.
